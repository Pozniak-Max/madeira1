"The Exiles"

A compelling historical drama set in Madeira during the mid-1800s, based on the extraordinary true story of Dr. Robert Reid Kalley and his followers, who became the Madeiran exiles. The film will immerse audiences in the captivating world of religious persecution, resilience, and the pursuit of freedom, resonating with both faith-based and mainstream audiences.

With the aesthetic appeal of "The Mission" and an emphasis on authentic storytelling, "The Exiles" aims to inspire, educate, and entertain viewers worldwide. This executive summary plan outlines the project's vision, target audience, marketing strategy, funding strategy, and high-level estimates of the budget, as well as early financial projections. The summary also includes a funding plan with different levels of donating, funding, and partnering.

Story Synopsis

  • September 8, 1809
    The Life and Achievements of Dr. Robert Kalley
    Born in Glasgow on September 8, 1809, Robert Kalley, the son of a tea merchant, overcame family tragedy. He attended Glasgow Grammar School, studied Arts at Glasgow University from 1823, and pursued Medicine, receiving an MB ChB in Pharmacy and Surgery by 1829. After working as a ship's doctor and a General practitioner, he obtained his MD in 1838.
  • 1838
    Dr. Robert Kalley's Mission to Transform Madeira
    In 1838, Dr. Robert Kalley, a Scottish minister and physician, arrived on Madeira, finding poverty, illiteracy, and poor health. He established clinics and schools, teaching people to read with the Bible as the main text.
  • August 9, 1846
    Rescue at Sea: Dr. Kalley's Followers Find Refuge
    After a riot on August 9, 1846, in Madeira, two British transport ships fired cannons intermittently to protect themselves, allowing Dr. Kalley's followers to find refuge onboard.
  • August 23, 1846
    Madeiran Exiles Find Refuge in Central Illinois
    On August 23, 1846, the British ship "William of Glasgow" set sail for Trinidad with 211 passengers. In the subsequent weeks and months, more converts sought refuge, resulting in approximately 2,000 Madeiran Exiles relocating to Trinidad, Antigua, St Kitts, Demerara, and Jamaica. Among them, 1,000 found a welcoming home in central Illinois.
  • February 17, 1888
    Dr. Robert Kalley's Legacy
    Dr. Robert Kalley passed away on February 17, 1888, at his home, Campo Verde, Tipperlinn Road, in western Edinburgh. He is survived by his wife Sarah, who continued their work and supported the Help for Brazil charity. Dr. Kalley and his family are laid to rest in Dean Cemetery in western Edinburgh, with plaques from grateful parishioners in Madeira and Brazil adorning their hidden southern terrace grave.
